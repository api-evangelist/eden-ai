The article argues that Europe's AI sovereignty strategy has fixated on building a European foundation model and owning European compute while overlooking the orchestration layer that controls data flows and model selection. It positions this routing infrastructure as the most cost-effective sovereignty investment, letting European enterprises maintain data residency and regulatory compliance while still accessing global AI capabilities.
